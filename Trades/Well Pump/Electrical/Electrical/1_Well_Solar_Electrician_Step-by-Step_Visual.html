Part 1 — Solar DC Wiring (Panels to Vault)
Location: Solar array at fence line → underground vault near wellhead
Step 1
Safety lockout
Before touching any terminals, verify all panel output is isolated. Cover panels with opaque material or work at dawn/dusk. Confirm 0V DC at panel terminals with a meter before proceeding.

Step 2
Verify panel string voltage and current
Measure and document: open-circuit voltage (Voc), short-circuit current (Isc), and operating voltage (Vmp) for each string. Record on the as-built sheet at the end of this guide. This is a required deliverable.

Step 3
Install PV combiner box at array
Mount PJERJZN 1-string PV combiner box (1000VDC, 40A MCB, 20-40KA SPD) within 10 ft of the array. This single unit combines the DC disconnect, fuse, and lightning arrestor/SPD. Connect MC4 cables from panel to combiner box input. DC output cable runs to vault charge controller. Bond combiner box ground terminal to array frame ground and grounding electrode conductor (GEC). Confirm green SPD indicator is lit before energizing system.
Step 4
Run DC cable from array to vault
Use USE-2 or PV wire rated for outdoor/direct burial. Run in 1" conduit where exposed. Conduit must be sunlight-resistant (gray PVC or metal) above grade. Bury per NEC 300.5 — minimum 24" depth for PV DC circuits in conduit. Seal conduit entry points.
[IMAGE 4: Conduit run / trench / entry seal]

Step 5
Verify combiner box breaker is off before connecting to charge controller
The PV combiner box already contains the fuse and 40A DC breaker. Before connecting DC output cable to the Victron MPPT charge controller, confirm the combiner box breaker is in the OFF position. Turn on only after all vault connections are complete and verified.

Step 6
Label all DC conductors
Red = positive, Black = negative. Mark voltage and current at each termination point. Apply SOLAR DC — DO NOT ENERGIZE labels at both ends.

Part 2 — Victron System & Battery Bank (In Vault)
Location: Underground precast concrete vault, near wellhead
Step 7
Inspect vault environment
Verify vault is dry, ventilated, and accessible. Confirm watertight hatch seals properly. Ventilation is required for battery off-gassing — do not seal vault airtight.
[IMAGE 7: Vault interior / ventilation]
Drop your image here
Step 8
Mount Victron charge controller
Mount MPPT charge controller on vault wall. Allow 4" clearance on all sides for airflow. Victron SmartSolar MPPT recommended — confirm model with RAC before ordering if not yet procured.
Step 9
Install battery bank
Install battery bank per design spec. If lithium (LiFePO4): confirm BMS is present and wired. If AGM: ensure batteries are vented. Connect batteries in series/parallel to achieve design voltage (likely 24V or 48V — confirm with RAC). Torque all terminals to spec. Apply anti-corrosion compound.
Step 10
Wire charge controller to battery bank
Connect MPPT output to battery bank: positive through a DC fuse sized per controller output rating, then to battery positive bus. Negative direct to battery negative bus. Follow Victron wiring sequence: batteries first, then PV input — NEVER connect PV before batteries.
[IMAGE 10: Controller-to-battery wiring]
Drop your image here
Step 11
Connect DC input from solar array
Connect the DC cable from Step 1-5 to the MPPT PV input terminals. Verify polarity before connecting. Confirm voltage reading on controller display matches documented string voltage.
[IMAGE 11: PV input connected / display]
Drop your image here
Step 12
Install and wire Victron inverter
Mount Victron MultiPlus-II 24/3000/70-50 2x120V (split phase) inverter in vault. Connect DC input from battery bank: positive through main DC fuse/breaker (250A per Victron spec for 3000VA/24V), then to inverter DC positive. Negative to inverter DC negative. Keep DC cable runs as short as possible — use 2/0 AWG or per Victron MultiPlus-II wiring guide to minimize voltage drop.
Step 13
Wire inverter AC output to VFD Commander Pro
Run AC output from Victron MultiPlus-II 2x120V to the existing VFD Commander Pro AC input. Wire L1 and L2 from inverter output to VFD AC input terminals for 240V split phase. The VFD is already installed — connect to its AC input terminals only. Do not open or modify the VFD enclosure beyond the AC input wiring. Confirm 240V across L1 and L2 before energizing VFD.

Step 14
Ground fault protection
Install ground fault protection device (GFPD) per NEC 690. Bond all metal enclosures, conduit, and equipment to grounding electrode system. Document ground rod location and resistance reading.

Step 15
Label all circuits
Label every breaker, fuse, conductor, and enclosure. Minimum labels: SOLAR INPUT, BATTERY BANK, INVERTER OUTPUT TO VFD, DC DISCONNECT, SYSTEM GROUND. Use UV-resistant labels.

Step 16
System commissioning — verify end-to-end
Energize in sequence: (1) batteries, (2) charge controller with PV, (3) inverter. Confirm charge controller shows PV input and charging. Confirm inverter output voltage is correct. Confirm VFD receives power. Document all readings. Do NOT test pump operation — pump and VFD are already commissioned by RAC.

Part 3 — Booster Pump Motor (At House)
Location: House pressure station — Grundfos booster pump
Step 17
Confirm power source
Determine if booster pump runs on grid power or solar/inverter output. Confirm with RAC before running circuits. This guide assumes a dedicated circuit from the house panel (grid-tied) unless RAC specifies otherwise.
[IMAGE 17: Power source / panel]
Drop your image here
Step 18
Install dedicated circuit and disconnect
Run a dedicated circuit from the house panel to the pressure station location. Install a clearly labeled disconnect within sight of the pump. Size wire and breaker per Grundfos motor nameplate (HP, FLA, voltage).
[IMAGE 18: Disconnect / circuit]

Step 19
Wire pressure switch (40/60 PSI)
Wire the pressure switch in series with the booster pump motor circuit. Pressure switch cuts pump on at 40 PSI, off at 60 PSI. Confirm switch is rated for motor load amperage. Use weatherproof enclosure if in an unconditioned space.
[IMAGE 19: Pressure switch wired]
Drop your image here
Step 20
Wire Grundfos booster pump motor
Connect motor leads per Grundfos wiring diagram (inside motor junction box). Confirm voltage configuration matches supply (120V or 240V). Torque terminals to spec. Seal motor junction box.
[IMAGE 20: Motor junction box]
Drop your image here
Step 21
Test booster pump operation
Energize circuit. Confirm pump starts when pressure drops below 40 PSI and stops at 60 PSI. Verify no overload trip on breaker. Check for unusual noise or vibration. Document operating pressure range and motor amperage draw under load.
